**Abstract**

Existing forensic tools for detecting synthetic or manipulated media are empirically grounded: they learn statistical signatures of known generators, fail on unseen distributions, and carry no analytical characterisation of their own failure modes. This paper describes a fundamentally different instrument. The core claim is that physical capture creates measurable mathematical coupling between independent physical projections of the same file — and that synthetic generation, having no single physical cause, cannot reproduce this coupling. The instrument extracts three reads from any file — Energy (noise residual), Space (geometric distortion), and Time (clock timing) — and measures coupling between them using the TwoNN intrinsic dimensionality estimator. Four coupling gap numbers are produced. All four large means single physical cause: file is real. Any gap collapsing means the causal chain broke: file is manipulated. All four near zero means no physical cause: file is fully synthetic. The instrument applies identically to images, audio, and video without modification. Every component traces to validated literature across six independent fields. No new mathematics, no training data, no empirical tuning. The failure modes are analytically derived from rate-distortion theory and the TwoNN reliability characterisation — not discovered by testing.

**Plain language summary**

Every photograph, audio recording, and video captured by a real device carries an invisible physical signature — a mathematical trace left by the fact that a single physical event caused all parts of the file simultaneously. Synthetic files generated by AI have no such single cause. Each part of a synthetic file is produced independently, and the trace is absent. This paper describes a tool that reads that trace directly, producing four numbers for any file. The numbers require no training data, work on any generator ever built or yet to be built, and have known, analytically derived limits. The tool applies identically to images, audio, and video.

## 1. Introduction

The problem of distinguishing real from synthetic digital files has attracted significant research effort. Existing approaches fall into two categories: artifact detection and generator fingerprinting. Artifact detection identifies statistical irregularities introduced by specific generation pipelines. Generator fingerprinting identifies signatures characteristic of known models. Both approaches share a structural weakness: they are empirically grounded. They work until a generator falls outside their training distribution. When challenged — in court, by a journalist, by a regulator — the honest answer is always: this generator we had not seen before.

This weakness is not an engineering failure. It is a consequence of the epistemological position these tools occupy. They answer the question: does this file look like files I have been trained to recognise as synthetic? That question has no analytical answer. It can only be answered empirically, which means it can always be falsified by a new generator.

This paper occupies a different epistemological position. The question it answers is: does this file carry the mathematical trace of a single physical cause? That question has an analytical answer. Physical causality either produced the file or it did not. No generator, however sophisticated, can fake a physical cause it does not have. This is not a claim about what generators currently do. It is a claim about what physical causality is.

The instrument described here is the operationalisation of that claim.

## 2. The Core Insight

Independent processes sharing a single physical cause leave a measurable mathematical trace of that shared cause.

This sentence is the complete theoretical foundation. Everything that follows either derives from it or validates one of its components.

When a camera captures an image, a single physical event — photons striking a sensor — simultaneously produces correlated structure in the noise field, the geometric distortion field, and the row-level timing field. These three projections are not independent. They share a cause. That shared cause is mathematically visible as reduced joint dimensionality relative to the sum of marginal dimensionalities.

When a generative model produces an image, no single physical event causes all three projections simultaneously. Each projection is produced by a separate computational process. The processes may individually resemble real projections. But they have no shared cause. The joint dimensionality does not reduce. The mathematical trace is absent.

The instrument measures the presence or absence of that trace.

## 3. Four Foundational Facts

The instrument rests on four facts. Each is independently established in existing literature. None requires the others to be true.

**Fact A: Physical capture creates inter-read coupling.**
A single physical capture event produces correlated structure across independent physical projections of the same event. This is a claim about physical reality. It is established by the physics of sensor capture and is the basis of the PRNU camera identification literature.

**Fact B: TwoNN measures joint dimensionality reduction.**
The TwoNN intrinsic dimensionality estimator, as proven by Facco et al. (2017), measures the intrinsic dimensionality of a dataset using only two nearest neighbours. Joint dimensionality minus the sum of marginal dimensionalities is a proxy for mutual information between projections. Low joint dimensionality relative to marginals means shared cause. This is a mathematical property of the estimator, not an empirical observation.

**Fact C: Energy, Space, and Time are orthogonal physical dimensions.**
The three reads — noise residual (Energy), geometric distortion (Space), and clock timing (Time) — inhabit orthogonal physical dimensions. This orthogonality is a property of physical reality, not of the extraction method. Orthogonality guarantees that TwoNN, which requires independent projections to measure coupling correctly, is operating on valid inputs.

**Fact D: Faithful processing and coupling preservation are the same property.**
A processing pipeline that faithfully reproduces the statistical properties of its input necessarily preserves coupling, because coupling is a defining statistical property of real signals. This identity is established by the combination of rate-distortion theory (Shannon, 1959) and neural network training distribution literature. It means coupling survives any real-world pipeline — compression, re-encoding, format conversion — up to the analytically characterised rate-distortion floor.

From these four facts, everything else derives:

- From A + C: the reads are independently coupled, making TwoNN valid
- From A + D: coupling survives any faithful pipeline
- From B + coupling: gap magnitude directly indicates physical cause presence
- From A + definition of synthetic generation: false negatives are impossible by definition
- From D: the calibration experiment dissolves into a literature review — every fidelity measurement ever published is simultaneously a calibration measurement for this instrument

## 4. Six Reframings

The novel contribution of this paper is not a new mathematical result. It is the recognition that six existing results across six independent fields were each independently describing the same underlying structure — and that assembled together they constitute a complete instrument for physical causality verification.

Each reframing takes an existing result, discards its original application, and retains its mathematical structure for a new purpose.

**Reframing 1: Fridrich et al. (2006) — PRNU camera identification**
*Original framing:* each camera has a unique noise fingerprint usable for device identification.
*Reframing:* the noise fingerprint is the Energy domain read. Camera identification is irrelevant. What matters is that this extraction is stable, validated, and carries structured physical signal. The validation is inherited; the application is discarded.

**Reframing 2: Facco et al. (2017) — TwoNN intrinsic dimensionality estimator**
*Original framing:* estimate the intrinsic dimensionality of a high-dimensional dataset efficiently using only two nearest neighbours.
*Reframing:* joint dimensionality minus marginal dimensionalities is a mutual information proxy. Low joint dimensionality means shared cause. The estimator was built to characterise dataset geometry. It is used here to measure physical causality. The mathematical structure is identical; the interpretation is entirely different.

**Reframing 3: Multi-parametric MRI literature — cross-map consistency**
*Original framing:* independent MRI modalities of the same tissue should be consistent because they share a physical cause. Inconsistency indicates pathology.
*Reframing:* independent physical reads of the same file should be consistent because they share a physical cause. Inconsistency indicates synthetic origin. The diagnostic logic is transplanted from biological tissue to digital files. The structure is identical.

**Reframing 4: Shannon (1959) — rate-distortion theory**
*Original framing:* there is a fundamental tradeoff between compression and signal fidelity. Below a certain rate, information is irreversibly lost.
*Reframing:* the coupling signal has a floor determined by rate-distortion bounds. This gives the operating envelope analytically. The floor is not discovered by testing. Shannon already computed it.

**Reframing 5: Neural network training distribution literature**
*Original framing:* models learn to reproduce statistical properties of their training data faithfully.
*Reframing:* if training data contains physical coupling, the pipeline preserves it — because destroying coupling would mean failing to reproduce a defining statistical property of real signals. Faithful processing and coupling preservation are the same property. This closes the proof that coupling survives any real pipeline. This holds across both adversarial pipelines, where fidelity is an emergent property of the generator-discriminator game (Goodfellow et al., 2014), and explicit reconstruction objectives, where fidelity is the direct training target (Ho et al., 2020). Together these two architectures cover the full landscape of modern generative models. Neither can destroy coupling without failing its own training objective.

**Reframing 6: Pipeline fidelity literature — PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS**
*Original framing:* measure how much perceptual quality a pipeline preserves at different compression levels.
*Reframing:* these curves are simultaneously calibration curves for the instrument. Pipeline fidelity and coupling preservation being the same property means every fidelity measurement ever published is a calibration measurement. The calibration experiment dissolves into a literature review.

The six reframings together constitute one claim: a file's physical origin is verifiable by measuring mutual information between independent physical projections, and every component needed to do this already exists in validated literature.

## 5. The Instrument

### 5.1 Three Reads

Three physical quantities are extracted from any file:

**Energy read — noise residual**
The noise residual after wavelet denoising. Validated by Fridrich et al. (2006) for images. The same physics governs preamp noise in audio and sensor noise in video frames.

**Space read — geometric distortion**
Deviation of detected edges from ideal straight lines, representing lens distortion. For audio, deviation of frequency response from flat reference, representing microphone nonlinearity. For video, per-frame lens distortion with cross-frame consistency measurement.

**Time read — clock timing**
For images: row-level noise correlation from CMOS rolling shutter timing, characterised within the PRNU forensics literature. For audio: Allan deviation of sample timestamps via allantools. For video: inter-frame interval jitter extracted via ffprobe.

### 5.2 Four Coupling Gaps

Three marginal dimensionalities are computed once:

```
d_noise     = TwoNN(noise_read).dimension_
d_geometry  = TwoNN(geometry_read).dimension_
d_clock     = TwoNN(clock_read).dimension_
```

Four joint gaps are computed:

```
gap(noise, geometry)  = TwoNN([noise, geometry]).dimension_ - d_noise - d_geometry
gap(noise, clock)     = TwoNN([noise, clock]).dimension_   - d_noise - d_clock
gap(geometry, clock)  = TwoNN([geometry, clock]).dimension_ - d_geometry - d_clock
gap(all three)        = TwoNN([noise, geometry, clock]).dimension_ - d_noise - d_geometry - d_clock
```

Each gap is the difference between joint dimensionality and the sum of marginal dimensionalities. Positive and large means coupled — shared physical cause. Near zero means decoupled — causal chain absent or broken.

### 5.3 Verdict

**All four gaps large** → single physical cause throughout → file is real

**Any gap near zero** → causal chain broke at some location → file is manipulated → binary search activates

**All four gaps near zero** → no physical cause anywhere → file is fully synthetic

The threshold is set by the pipeline fidelity literature (Reframing 6). It is not empirically tuned.

### 5.4 Binary Search

When any gap collapses, binary search localises the break:

1. Divide the file into four regions (spatial quadrants for images, temporal segments for audio and video)
2. Recompute joint_all_gap for each region
3. Recurse into the region with lowest gap
4. Stop when region produces fewer than 200 points per read — the TwoNN reliability floor from Facco et al. (2017)

Output: location of break, which pair broke, magnitude of collapse, which pairs survived.

### 5.5 Media Type Closure

The four foundational facts contain no reference to media type. Energy, Space, and Time are physical dimensions, not media-specific quantities. The instrument applies identically across images, audio, and video because the reads inhabit the same physical dimensions regardless of how the file encodes them — spatially for images, temporally for audio, both for video.

The instrument does not extend to audio and video. It was never about images specifically. Media type is the dimension the reads inhabit, not a parameter of the instrument.

## 6. Three-Sided Verdict

The instrument produces three states, not two. This is not a design choice. It is a consequence of what physical causality actually is.

**Side 1 — Real:** physical causality present throughout. All four gaps large.

**Side 2 — Fully synthetic:** physical causality absent throughout. All four gaps near zero. No single physical cause exists anywhere in the file.

**Side 3 — Manipulated:** physical causality present then broken. Some gaps collapse at a specific location. A real file was modified — spliced, edited, partially regenerated. The instrument not only detects this but localises where the causal chain broke.

Most forensic tools operate on a two-sided coin: real or fake. The three-sided structure follows necessarily from measuring physical causality rather than pattern matching against known generators.

## 7. Limitations

The limitations of this instrument are analytically derived, not empirically discovered. This is stated explicitly because it distinguishes the instrument's failure characterisation from that of empirical tools.

**Rate-distortion floor.** Below the compression rate at which coupling signal is irreversibly lost, the instrument cannot distinguish real from synthetic. This floor is computed by Shannon (1959), not discovered by testing. Files compressed below this floor are outside the instrument's operating envelope, and the envelope boundary is known analytically.

**TwoNN reliability floor.** Below 200 sample points per read, the TwoNN estimator becomes unreliable, as characterised by Facco et al. (2017). Binary search stops at this boundary. This is a mathematical property of the estimator, not a tunable parameter.

**Extraction implementation fidelity.** The theory requires that each extraction function reads what the physics says it should read. This is an engineering constraint, not a theoretical weakness. If the instrument appears not to work, the first diagnostic question is whether the extraction is correctly reading its physical quantity. This constraint does not grow with new generators or new media types. It is a fixed implementation problem per domain, bounded in scope.

There is no failure mode of the form: a new generator appeared that this instrument cannot detect. The instrument does not detect generators. It detects the presence or absence of physical causality. No generator produces physical causality. The escape hatch available to empirical tools does not exist here. If the instrument fails, one of the four foundational facts must be false — and each of those facts is either a mathematical identity or a physical law.
